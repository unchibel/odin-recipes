Greek Gigantes Plaki

Description

Comforting and buttery gigantes plaki, or Greek giant baked beans, with tomatoes and olive oil make a satisfying vegetarian dinner any night of the week. Or, you can serve them at room temperature over crusty bread as an appetizer!

Ingredients

  • 400g dried butter beans
  • 3 tablespoons Greek extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato purée
  • 800g ripe tomatoes, skins removed, roughly chopped
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano
  • pinch ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons chopped flat-leaf parsley

Steps

  1. Soak the beans overnight in plenty of water.
  2. Drain, rinse, then place in a pan covered with water.
  3. Bring to the boil, reduce the heat, then simmer for approx 50 mins until slightly tender but not soft. Drain, then set aside.
  4. Heat oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4.
  5. Heat the olive oil in a large frying pan, tip in the onion and garlic, then cook over a medium heat for 10 mins until softened but not browned.
  6. Add the tomato purée, cook for a further min, add remaining ingredients, then simmer for 2-3 mins.
  7. Season generously, then stir in the beans.
  8. Tip into a large ovenproof dish, then bake for approximately 1 hr, uncovered and without stirring, until the beans are tender.
  9. Allow to cool, then scatter with parsley and drizzle with a little more olive oil to serve.
